ACCA Qualification in a nutshell

The Association of Chartered Certified Accountants (ACCA) qualification is a globally recognised accountancy qualification. It is a badge of quality and professionalism and ensures that your knowledge and skills are of the highest professional standard.

Steps

To become an ACCA Chartered Accountant you will need to complete:

  • Foundation level papers
  • Professional level papers
  • The Practical Experience Requirement (three years of work experience)
  • A Professional Ethics module

Foundation Level

The foundation level consists of nine papers (F1-F9), all of which are compulsory. Depending on your previous education you may qualify for exemptions from some of the papers at this level

Our full-time foundation level programmes give you exemptions from these exams. Our part-time courses prepare you to sit these papers with the ACCA.

Professional Level

The professional level consists of three compulsory papers (P1-P3) and four optional papers (P4-P7) from which you will need to complete two papers.

Both our full-time and part-time professional level courses prepare you to sit these papers with the ACCA.

Practical Experience Requirement (PER)

To complete PER you must complete 36 months' employment in an accounting or finance-related role. During this time you will need to record and report on your progress and acheive a number of performance objectives.

The three years can consist of full-time or part-time work, temporary positions and internship as long as they meet the ACCA requirements.

Professional Ethics module

The Professional Ethics module consists on several self-tests which require you to reflect on your ethical behaviour. You will gain access to the Professional Ethics module with the ACCA after you complete papers F1-F3.
